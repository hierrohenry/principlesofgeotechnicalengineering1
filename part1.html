Principles of Geotechnical Engineering

What is Geotechnical Engineering

Soil is defined as loose aggregates of mineral grains, with liquid and gas in the empty spaces between the solid particles. Soil is often used to support structures in Civil and Structural Engineering. The properties of soil govern their strength and behaviour. Properties of soils include origin, grain-size distribution, porosity, compressibility, shear strength and load bearing capacity.
